# Review: mixture-of-experimenters

## Verdict
**Accept with major revisions.** This is an honest, well-instrumented paper with a genuine conceptual contribution — the *matched control* is what separates an "experiment" from a "perturbation," and the paper demonstrates cleanly that the control's value is conditional on the intervention's specificity. The numbers reproduce, the citations are real, and the simulation does what the prose says. The single most serious problem is rhetorical inflation of the central comparison: "a label-free signal beats a *supervised* probe" conflates *supervision* with *information access*. The experiments win because they run 3–5 counterfactual forward passes and watch the output move; the probe sees one frozen activation. That is a multi-pass-causal-probe-beats-single-pass-readout result, not a labels-are-useless result, and the framing (abstract + §4) overstates it. A second, near-equal problem: the abstract spins the generalization result, which actually shows the signal *stops* beating confidence once the model must find its own evidence.

## Claim grounding

- **"separates correct from incorrect answers with AUROC 0.967 … (0.956 for two) … 0.786 … 0.784 … 0.602"** → **supported.** Matches `c1_..._verification.json` exactly (0.96658, 0.95590, 0.78568, 0.78411, 0.60184).

- **"It beats the supervised probe, which uses labels, by [+0.332, +0.377]: the probe reaches only 0.602, because a readout trained on noisy activations to predict correctness does not generalise"** → **overstated, and partly unsupported.** Three problems. (1) The probe is *linear* and trained on a **50/50 retrieval+parity mixture** (`fixtures.py:76` calls `correctness_probe` with the default `frac_retrieval=0.5`) but evaluated on **retrieval-only** — a distribution shift baked into the comparison; parity answers (EVEN/ODD) live in a different geometry from value-token answers, diluting the probe. (2) "**does not generalise**" is asserted but never measured — no train-vs-test AUROC gap is reported; 0.602 is equally consistent with the probe simply *underfitting* (linear-undecodability of correctness from a 64-d noisy residual). (3) Most importantly, the probe sees one activation while each experiment runs multiple intervention passes; the win is about *information*, not *supervision*.

- **"whether an answer survives intervention on its own evidence is a far stronger label-free signal of correctness than how confident the model is"** → **supported on these two tasks**, but the generalization beyond single-token in-context retrieval is unestablished (see Argument).

- **"it survives identifying the evidence by causal attribution rather than being given it, though the margin then narrows with attribution quality"** (abstract) → **overstated.** `c3_..._verification.json`: attributed swap AUROC = 0.683 vs **entropy 0.719**; `attributed_minus_entropy` point = **−0.037**, CI [−0.105, +0.030]. The signal does not merely "narrow" — it **drops below confidence** and loses the property that is the paper's entire selling point. §5/§6 state this honestly ("no longer beats entropy"); the abstract does not.

- **"The matched control is necessary where the intervention has a non-specific component (ablation … +0.016, CI95 [+0.010, +0.022]) and inert where the intervention is intrinsically specific (… +0.000)"** → **fully supported.** Matches `c1a_ablate` (0.01596, [0.01005, 0.02228]) and `c1a_swap` (0.00048, [−0.0043, +0.0053]). This is the paper's cleanest, best-grounded result.

- **Pythia "AUROC 0.841 against 0.719 for entropy"** → **supported with a trivial rounding slip:** `c2` `neg_entropy` = 0.72007, which rounds to **0.720**, not 0.719 (Table 2 and abstract both print 0.719; logit margin 0.71898 → 0.719 is correct). Also Table 2's entropy CI lower bound prints 0.668 where the JSON is 0.6686 (→0.669), and §5's control CI prints [−0.011,…] where the JSON is −0.0104 (→−0.010). All cosmetic; none load-bearing.

## Argument

- **§4 (the headline comparison).** The "label-free beats supervised" framing is the weakest joint in the paper. The honest claim is *multi-pass causal probing beats a single-pass linear readout*. The probe is additionally handicapped by a mixed-task training distribution and an unmeasured "does not generalise" assertion. Gap: the comparison is not apples-to-apples, and the prose leans on the apples-to-oranges reading.

- **§4 (why the toy numbers are so high) — the deepest unstated weakness.** On this task, `correct ≡ yhat == queried value`, and the swap experiment measures essentially "does the output follow when the queried value token is overwritten" — i.e., "does the model read the queried binding." On a single-token lookup, *reading the queried binding* and *being correct* are nearly the same event by construction. So AUROC ~0.90–0.96 partly reflects a near-tautology between the experiment and the task's correctness criterion, not evidence that the method discriminates correctness where grounding and correctness can diverge (multi-evidence reasoning, aggregation, arithmetic). The paper flags the *evidence-identification* bottleneck (§6) but never this tighter one. Gap: the toy cannot, even in principle, separate "grounding tracks correctness" from "the task makes them coincide."

- **Abstract vs §5/§6 (generalization).** Already covered: the abstract hides a sign flip. Gap: the most load-bearing limitation is disclosed everywhere except the place readers anchor on.

- **External validity (§5–6).** One hand-built toy + one 160M model, both single-token in-context retrieval; n=400 with wide CIs on Pythia; the control's marginal value on Pythia is *not* significant ([−0.011, +0.070]). The paper is genuinely well-hedged about this in §6, so it is a boundary, not a fatal flaw — but combined with the near-tautology point above, "the mechanism is not an artifact of the toy" (§5) is doing more work than two retrieval tasks can bear.

- **Title vs. delivery.** The "mixture" is an unweighted z-score *sum* of 2–3 hand-designed probes — no routing, no learned weighting. The paper is candid ("not a routing of compute, but a panel"), but the MoE-analogy branding promises more than a fixed sum of three signals delivers.

**Genuinely strong:** the experiment-vs-steer distinction (answer-agnostic intervention with a prediction, vs. pushing toward a chosen target) is a real and crisp conceptual contribution; the conditional-value-of-control result (C1a) is a clean, falsifiable, well-supported finding; the label-free discipline is implemented correctly (signals use only `yhat` and input structure; labels enter solely at AUROC scoring; the z-score is transductive but monotone-safe and honestly described); attribution-by-ablation / test-by-swap correctly avoids circularity.

## Prose and insight
**Alive:** the opening — "The word 'expert' in mixture-of-experts is a fossil" — earns its place and sets up the real idea. "Pushing toward a conclusion is not an experiment. It presupposes the answer that an experiment is meant to put at risk" is the paper's best sentence and its genuine thesis. The discussion's framing of swap-as-positive-test and ablate-as-negative-test, and the "a control is required when the intervention is non-specific" principle, are crisp and earned. This is not AI-slop; the argument moves.

**Dead/forgettable:** parts of §2 are a list of "X does this; Y does that" with little synthesis; the conclusion mostly restates the abstract. The phrase "the control this whole literature lacks" (§4) is a swagger the evidence (two retrieval tasks) doesn't fully fund.

**Genuine contribution:** yes — the matched control as the demarcation between experiment and perturbation, plus the empirical demonstration that its value is conditional on intervention specificity. That is a real, transferable idea, and it is the thing to lead with, more than the (overstated) "beats a supervised probe" line.

## Top fixes
1. **Reframe the headline comparison.** Replace "beats a supervised probe" / "a label-free signal beats a supervised one" (abstract, §4, §6) with the accurate claim: a *multi-pass causal probe* beats a *single-pass linear readout* and the confidence signals. State explicitly that the experiments have more information (interventional forward passes), not just no labels.
2. **Fix the abstract's generalization sentence.** Say plainly what §5 already says: under self-attribution at this model's quality (0.488), the attributed signal (0.683) **no longer beats entropy** (0.719). "the margin then narrows" must become "the advantage over confidence disappears."
3. **Strengthen or caveat the probe baseline.** Train the probe on **retrieval-only** at the eval noise (remove the 50/50 distribution shift), and report its **train AUROC** so "does not generalise" is either substantiated or dropped. Add a non-linear (small MLP) probe; if it also loses, the claim is far more convincing. If you keep the linear probe, label it "linear correctness probe" and drop "does not generalise."
4. **Disclose the toy's grounding≈correctness coincidence.** Add a paragraph (§4 or §6): because the task is a single-token lookup, "uses the queried binding" and "is correct" nearly coincide, which inflates the toy AUROC and limits what it shows. This is the most important missing caveat.
5. **Tone down "the control this whole literature lacks" and the MoE branding.** Acknowledge the "mixture" is a fixed unweighted z-score sum of 2–3 hand-designed probes; frame routing/learned aggregation as future work (you already gesture at this — make it explicit where the title's promise is set).
6. **Citation hygiene.** Cite ATLAS as "Nguyen & Le (2026)" and list both authors; fix the CausalGaze gloss ("hallucination/factuality detection," not "unfaithful explanations") and the Xu gloss ("contextual interference," not "paraphrase").
7. **Cosmetic numbers.** Correct the Pythia entropy AUROC to 0.720 in Table 2 and the abstract, and round (not truncate) the CI bounds for consistency.
